# GTM.md

## Exact Target User

Not "startups." The specific person is: **a CTO or senior engineering manager at a seed-to-Series A startup (10–80 employees) who directly controls the software budget and recently had a finance or CEO conversation about burn rate.**

They are not the founder (too busy, delegates this). They are not a junior dev (no budget authority). They are the person who gets a Slack message from the CEO saying "can we audit our SaaS spend?" and then has to produce an answer by Friday.

Specific job titles: VP Engineering, Head of Engineering, CTO (at 15–40 person companies where the CTO still writes code), Senior Engineering Manager.

Company stage: post-product-market-fit, pre-Series B. At this stage they have 5–15 SaaS subscriptions, a real monthly bill ($2k–$20k/mo on software), and a finance person who is now paying attention.

## First 100 Users in 30 Days, $0 Budget

**Week 1: Hacker News Show HN post**
"Show HN: I built a free tool to audit your team's AI spend" with a genuine write-up of the problem, the approach, and a live link. HN Show HN threads regularly drive 500–2000 visitors for a well-framed post. Target: 40–60 audit completions from this alone.

**Week 2: Targeted Reddit posts**
Post in r/ExperiencedDevs and r/startups — not as spam, but as a genuine "I built this because I was frustrated with X" post. Reddit converts well when the post is honest about what the tool doesn't do. Target: 20–30 completions.

**Week 3: Cold DMs on X**
Find threads where engineers are asking "is Cursor worth it" or "ChatGPT Team vs Plus." Reply with a direct link. 50 targeted replies to relevant conversations. Not broadcast, surgical. Target: 15–20 completions.

**Week 4: Leverage shareable results**
Users who complete audits showing >$500 savings are highly likely to screenshot and share. One viral tweet from a founder saying "this tool just told me my team is wasting $800/mo on AI tools" can drive 200+ visits in 24 hours. Design the results page so it's screenshot-worthy.
